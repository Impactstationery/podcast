About Laura

"I’ve helped over 200 coaches and therapists turn their method into a physical product — journals, planners, cards — and together we’ve produced more than 500,000 units, without a publisher, a bookstore, or a massive following."

Laura got here the way most good ideas start: through a personal problem. Diagnosed with ADHD as an adult, she couldn’t find a planner that actually worked for her brain — so she made one. That product went on to sell over 160,000 units. Then came another, and another. What started as a personal solution became a manufacturing collective and a methodology.

Before stationery, Laura spent years at Amazon and built her own eCommerce business, developing a deep fluency in product development and manufacturing that most coaches and consultants never acquire. That combination — expert methodology and manufacturing know-how — is what makes Impact Stationery unusual.

Today, while most business coaches are telling their clients to build courses and digital products, Laura makes the opposite case. We’re watching a cultural shift: in a world saturated with content, PDFs, and AI-generated everything, a physical product cuts through in ways that digital simply can’t. Esther Perel turned her relationship method into a card game. Steven Bartlett built The 1% Diary from his podcast’s core idea. James Clear created an Atomic Habits journal. The experts who endure create things you can hold.

Laura’s flagship course and her own Productize Your Method Planner are proof of concept — she doesn’t just teach this, she does it herself.
